The problem

Across the system, every actor is optimising for the wrong thing.

Platforms

Profit from quality volume

More listings, more take-rate.

Hosts

Optimised for identity occupancy

Calendars beat character.

Guests

Filtered by experience price

Cheapest wins the search.

A solution owned by one platform will always serve that platform first. The intelligence needs to sit above them.

European cities pushing back

"The problem is not just Paris. It is all of Europe."
— Anne Hidalgo, former Mayor of Paris

City by city

Barcelona

All tourist apartment licences expire 2028. 10,000+ homes returned to residential market.

Amsterdam

Airbnb listings fell 54% between 2019 and 2024.

Berlin

8,105 apartments returned to residential housing.

Paris

90-day annual cap since January 2025. Two hosts fined €80,000 and €150,000 in February 2026.

Spain

€64 million fine for Airbnb, December 2025. 65,122 unlicensed listings.

Athens

Ban on new short-term rental licences in central districts from January 2025.

Florence

Moratorium on new licences in historic centre. Protecting against "touristification."

Budapest

District referendum resulted in ban on short-term rentals from 2026.
